## TL;DR

Recommended action: **Option A** (below) immediately, then upgrade to **Option B** once you've verified NDA status on the named healthcare/pharma companies.

---

## Why the current section must change

Three problems, not just the inflated numbers:

### 1. The numbers are a trust bomb, not a trust signal
"500+ Happy Clients" and "1000+ Projects Done" are what every generic agency site claims. Sophisticated buyers categorize you as "random WordPress shop." Worse, a healthcare practice manager or pharma procurement lead will do basic due diligence, find no evidence of 1000 projects, and now you're not unimpressive — you're dishonest. For a voice AI product selling into HIPAA-regulated environments, this is a deal-killer.

### 2. Even accurate, they'd be the wrong numbers
"Team Members" and "Projects Done" are volume metrics. Volume isn't what Bytestreams sells. Depth is. A healthcare data engineer with 20 years at Stryker/Sterling/Sonafi is worth more than a 50-person generic agency — and the site should reflect that math.

### 3. The copy contradicts the positioning
"Passionate innovators dedicated to creating exceptional digital experiences" is 2014 web agency boilerplate. It says nothing about healthcare fluency, production AI systems, or 20 years of data engineering. The "Why Choose Us" cards say *"twenty years in the code, not the conference circuit"* — and this section says *"passionate innovators."* They contradict each other.

## Option B (upgrade path — ship once NDAs verified)

Same layout as Option A. Left column stays identical. Right column tiles become industry/client references instead of numeric claims.

### Right column tiles (alternate version)

| Position | Value | Label |
|---|---|---|
| Top-left | **Pharma** | Sterling Pharma, Sonafi |
| Top-right | **MedDevice** | Stryker Medical, Wright Medical |
| Bottom-left | **Healthcare** | Accredo Health |
| Bottom-right | **Data/Cloud** | 1.2 PB AWS Data Lake |

### Before shipping Option B — two risks to resolve

1. **Verify you can name companies publicly.** Some contracts have non-disclosure clauses that prohibit naming the client on marketing materials. Check every engagement before publishing.
2. **Watch the framing.** "Shipped production systems for" is safer than "Clients include." The former describes the work honestly whether it was direct or contractor-level; the latter implies a formal vendor relationship that may not match the actual engagement structure.

If NDA check is clean and framing is accurate, Option B is more powerful than Option A because concrete company names carry enormous credibility weight.

- You haven't finalized the credibility content

Better to have no section than one with false numbers. But this leaves real estate on the table that Option A could fill with zero risk.

## Visual / design notes

### Keep
- The two-column layout
- The dark gradient tile design
- The alternating gradient pattern (navy / black / navy / navy currently)
- The checkmark icon style

### Adjust
- **Number formatting:** "1.2 PB" may not fit at the same font size as "10+". Test for readability; may need slightly smaller font for the longer values.
- **Labels under numbers:** Current labels are short ("Years Experience"). New labels are longer ("Years Shipping Production Systems"). May need tighter line-height or slightly smaller font.
- **Tile proportions:** If "LLM Providers Unified in Our Open-Source Framework" wraps too aggressively, consider "9 LLM Providers in Our Open-Source Framework" as a shorter variant.

### Avoid
- Stock photos of diverse smiling people at laptops
- Abstract swoosh / "AI network" graphics behind the tiles
- Animated counter effects on the numbers (triggers skepticism)
