02Consent — How You Opt In

You will only receive SMS messages from a restaurant using DialTone if you have provided express consent through one of the following channels:

Voice consent during a phone order

When you call a restaurant that uses DialTone, our AI voice agent captures your SMS consent at the start of the call, before taking your order. This is because completing your order requires us to text you a Stripe payment link, and we won't take your order unless we have permission to send that text. The exact language the agent uses is:

Agent Script · Voice Consent "Thanks for calling [Restaurant Name]! I can take your order right now. Just so you know, to finish your order I'll need to text you a payment link from Stripe, and I'll also text you a confirmation when payment clears and another text when your order is ready. Message and data rates may apply, and you can reply STOP anytime to unsubscribe. Is it OK to text you at the number you're calling from?"

If you say yes, the agent will read back the last four digits of the number you're calling from (or a different number if you provide one) and confirm it's the right number for texts before proceeding. Your verbal consent is recorded, time-stamped, and retained as proof of opt-in.

If you say no, the agent will politely transfer you to restaurant staff so they can take your order directly. No SMS messages will be sent and no consent record will be created.

Website consent during online ordering

When you place an order on a restaurant's website that uses DialTone, you may see two consent checkboxes. The first is required to place the order (because we need to text you a payment link); the second is optional:

Required Checkbox · Transactional "Yes, text me a Stripe payment link, order confirmation, and pickup/delivery updates from [Restaurant Name]. Message frequency varies. Message and data rates may apply. Reply STOP to unsubscribe. See our Privacy Policy and SMS Terms."
Optional Checkbox · Promotional "Also text me occasional offers from [Restaurant Name], up to 2 per month. Reply STOP to unsubscribe."

Both checkboxes are unchecked by default. You must affirmatively check the required checkbox to place the order. The promotional checkbox is entirely optional and can be left unchecked. No pre-checked boxes, no opt-in bundled into unrelated agreements.

03What Messages You'll Receive

Opted-in customers may receive the following message types from the restaurant:

Type Purpose Frequency
Payment link A secure Stripe payment link to complete your order 1 per order
Payment confirmation Confirms your payment cleared and recaps the order 1 per order
Order ready / status update Notifies you when your order is ready for pickup or out for delivery 1–2 per order
Reservation reminder Reminds you of an upcoming reservation (where supported) 1 per reservation
Promotional Occasional offers or menu updates (only if you opted in to promotional messages) Up to 2 per month
HELP response Sent in reply to your HELP request As requested

Transactional messages (payment link, payment confirmation, order ready) are required to complete a voice order. If you decline consent during the call, the agent will transfer you to restaurant staff so your order can be taken directly — no SMS messages will be sent. Promotional messages are always optional and separately opted-in.

05How to Stop Receiving Messages

You can opt out at any time by replying any of the following keywords to any message:

  • STOP
  • END
  • CANCEL
  • UNSUBSCRIBE
  • QUIT

Once you opt out, you will receive one final confirmation message:

Opt-Out Confirmation "[Restaurant Name]: You've opted out and will no longer receive messages. To re-subscribe, reply START."

You will not receive further messages from that restaurant unless you explicitly opt back in. Opt-outs are processed immediately and automatically by DialTone's platform.

SMS messaging through DialTone is intended for recipients who are at least 18 years old, or who are old enough to consent to receive commercial SMS messages under applicable state law. 10Recording and Retention

Voice consents are recorded and retained as proof of opt-in. Consent records are kept for a minimum of four (4) years after the most recent message sent to the opted-in number, or longer if required by applicable law. Website consents are logged with timestamp, IP address, and the exact consent language displayed at the time of opt-in.
